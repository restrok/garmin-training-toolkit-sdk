# Running Training Plan Generation - Scientific Principles

A research-based guide for creating evidence-based running training plans.

---

## 1. Training Intensity Distribution

### The 80/20 Rule (Polarized Training)

Research consistently shows that elite endurance athletes distribute their training as:

| Zone | Distribution | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Low (Z1/Z2)** | **~80%** | Easy, conversational pace |
| **Threshold (Z3)** | **~0-5%** | Tempo pace (rarely spent here) |
| **High (Z4/Z5)** | **~15-20%** | Intervals, race pace |

**Source:** Frontiers in Physiology (2014), Sports Medicine (2024)

**Key finding:** Polarized training produces greater improvements in VO2max and performance compared to threshold-focused or high-volume training alone.

### Why Most Recreational Runners Train Too Hard

- Recreational runners often do 50-70% of training at moderate intensity
- This "gray zone" doesn't optimally stress either aerobic or anaerobic systems
- **Optimal:** Spend 80% below threshold, 20% above threshold

## 2. Heart Rate Zones

### Zone Calculation Methods

**Method 1: Percentage of Max Heart Rate (HRmax)**
```
HRmax = 220 - age (Tanaka: 208 - 0.7 × age)
```

**Method 2: Heart Rate Reserve (Karvonen)**
```
Target HR = ((HRmax - HRrest) × %intensity) + HRrest
```

### Standard 5-Zone Model

| Zone | Name | % HRmax | Feel | Training Effect |
|------|-------|---------|------|----------------|
| **Z1** | Recovery | 50-60% | Very easy | Active recovery |
| **Z2** | Aerobic | 60-70% | Conversational | Fat oxidation, capillary density |
| **Z3** | Tempo | 70-80% | Comfortably hard | Lactate clearance (rarely trained) |
| **Z4** | Threshold | 80-90% | Uncomfortable | Lactate threshold |
| **Z5** | VO2max | 90-100% | Very hard | VO2max, anaerobic capacity |

**Source:** American College of Sports Medicine

## 4. Periodization

### Classic Periodization Model

```
                    Peak
                   /    \
                  /      \
                 /        \
        ________/          \_______
       /                            \
      /                              \
_____/                                \_____
     
Base → Build → Peak → Race → Recovery
```

### Training Phases

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Intensity |
|-------|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Base** | 4-8 weeks | Aerobic foundation | 80-90% Z2 |
| **Build** | 4-6 weeks | Specific fitness | 70% Z2, 20% intervals |
| **Peak** | 2-4 weeks | Race simulation | 60% Z2, 30% threshold/VO2max |
| **Taper** | 1-2 weeks | Recovery | Reduce volume, maintain intensity |
| **Recovery** | 1 week | Rest | 50% volume |

### 4-Week Training Cycle

| Week | Load | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| Week 1 | Medium | Build fitness |
| Week 2 | High | Push adaptation |
| Week 3 | High | Consolidate |
| Week 4 | **Low (Deload)** | Recovery + supercompensation |

**Source:** Journal of Sports Medicine (2023)

## 5. Workout Types

### 1. Easy Runs (Zone 2)

- **Duration:** 30-90 minutes
- **Purpose:** Build aerobic base, capillary density, mitochondrial biogenesis
- **Feel:** Can hold conversation
- **Frequency:** 3-5x per week

### 2. Long Runs

- **Duration:** 60-180 minutes
- **Purpose:** Endurance, mental toughness, fat adaptation
- **Pace:** Zone 2 (very easy)
- **Frequency:** 1x per week

### 3. Tempo Runs (Threshold)

- **Duration:** 20-40 minutes at threshold
- **Purpose:** Raise lactate threshold
- **Feel:** "Comfortably hard" - can speak in short phrases
- **Frequency:** 1-2x per week (only for intermediate+)

### 4. Intervals (VO2max)

- **Work:** 3-5 minutes at 5K race pace (Z4/Z5)
- **Recovery:** 1-3 minutes easy
- **Reps:** 4-8
- **Purpose:** Improve VO2max
- **Frequency:** 1-2x per week

### 5. Speed/Fartlek

- **Duration:** 30 sec - 3 min bursts
- **Purpose:** Running economy, neuromuscular
- **Frequency:** 1x per week (advanced only)

## 6. Recovery Principles

### Deload Weeks

Every 4th week should reduce volume/intensity:

| Deload Type | Volume | Intensity |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Full deload** | -40-50% | Same |
| **Partial deload** | -20-30% | Same |
| **Intensity reduction** | Same | -30-40% |

**Source:** Runner's World, Breaking Muscle

### Recovery Indicators

| Good Recovery | Poor Recovery |
|--------------|---------------|
| RHR stable or lower | RHR elevated 5+ bpm |
| HRV stable or higher | HRV decreased |
| Good sleep quality | Poor sleep |
| Energetic feeling | Fatigue, heaviness |
| Resting HR zones normal | HR zones elevated |

### Recovery Timeline

| Training Type | Recovery Time |
|--------------|--------------|
| Easy run | 12-24 hours |
| Tempo run | 24-48 hours |
| Long run | 48-72 hours |
| Interval session | 48-72 hours |
| Race | 7-14 days |

## 7. Overtraining Prevention

### Signs of Overtraining

- Persistent elevated resting HR
- Decreased performance
- Increased perceived effort
- Mood disturbances
- Insomnia or poor sleep
- Frequent illness
- Injuries

### The February Pattern (Your Case)

- **Too much intensity** (avg HR 150-165)

- **Insufficient recovery**

**Recovery prescription:**

- 4-6 weeks base building (Zone 2 only)
- Gradual return to intensity

## 9. Training Plan Generation Rules

### Golden Rules for Plan Creation

1. **80/20 Distribution**
   - 80% of runs at Zone 2
   - 20% at Zone 4/5 (intervals, race pace)

2. **Progressive Overload**
   - Increase weekly volume by ≤10% per week
   - Increase intensity gradually
   - Every 4th week is deload

3. **Periodization**
   - Base → Build → Peak → Taper
   - Build aerobic base before adding intensity

4. **Recovery Priority**
   - No hard days back-to-back
   - Easy days truly easy
   - Sleep 7-9 hours minimum

5. **Individualization**
   - Adjust based on recovery markers
   - RHR, HRV, sleep quality
   - Adjust if injured or sick

## Summary: Plan Generation Algorithm

When creating a training plan, the system should:

1. **Assess current fitness** (VO2max, recent training load)
2. **Set clear goal** (5K, 10K, Half, Marathon)
3. **Apply periodization** (12-16 week cycle)
4. **Distribute intensity** (80% Z2, 20% high intensity)
5. **Include deload weeks** (every 4th week)
6. **Schedule recovery** (no consecutive hard days)
7. **Progress gradually** (+10% weekly volume max)
8. **Taper before race** (2-3 weeks reduction)
